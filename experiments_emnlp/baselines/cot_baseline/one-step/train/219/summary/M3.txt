The text discusses the British Mission during World War II, focusing on the development of the atomic bomb. It details the initial skepticism in the UK regarding the military application of uranium fission, the pivotal Frisch-Peierls memorandum predicting the feasibility of a uranium bomb, and the subsequent collaboration with the United States on the Manhattan Project. The British contribution to the project, the eventual successful independent nuclear weapons program, and the postwar cooperation between the US and UK on atomic energy are also highlighted.
