Mark Oliphant, a renowned physicist born in Australia, made significant contributions to nuclear physics, including splitting the atom and developing radar technology during World War II. Despite his involvement in the Manhattan Project, he later became a vocal advocate against nuclear weapons, emphasizing the peaceful use of atomic energy. Oliphant's humanitarian stance led him to join global discussions on nuclear disarmament. His legacy includes various honors and the establishment of scientific institutions in Australia. He passed away in 2000, leaving a lasting impact on the scientific community.
