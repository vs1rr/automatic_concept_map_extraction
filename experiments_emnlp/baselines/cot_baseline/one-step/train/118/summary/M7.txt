The Brougham Roman fort (Brocavum) and civil settlement, along with Brougham Castle, are designated as nationally important monuments. The fort and castle display significant historical and archaeological value, with well-preserved remains offering insights into Roman and medieval military occupation in northern England. The site's historical interest, documentation, and group value with other contemporary structures contribute to its significance. The history of the fort and castle, from Roman times to the medieval period, is detailed, including information on construction, occupation, and subsequent modifications. Various archaeological investigations have uncovered buried remains and artifacts, shedding light on the site's past.
