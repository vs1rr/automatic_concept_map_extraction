In 1833, significant events took place around the world. President Andrew Jackson opposed South Carolina's defiance, a new governor was appointed in Buenos Aires, the Kirtland Temple construction began, and Otto became the first modern King of Greece. Other events included the desecration of a grave leading to a revolt, a mutiny in Vietnam, British sovereignty over the Falkland Islands, a massive earthquake in Sumatra, and the first train ride by a U.S. President. Additionally, Admiral Charles Napier achieved victory in a naval battle, the Convention of 1833 in Mexican Texas began, the Chilean Constitution was promulgated, and the Slavery Abolition Act was passed in the British Empire. Lastly, the UK claimed sovereignty over the Falkland Islands, and Oberlin College was founded in Ohio.
