The Book of Kells, housed at Trinity College in Dublin, is a renowned illuminated manuscript created by Celtic monks around 800 AD. It contains the four Gospels of the New Testament and features intricate illustrations of humans, animals, and mythical creatures, all infused with Christian symbolism. The manuscript, written on calf vellum with iron gall ink and vibrant imported colors, attracts over 500,000 visitors annually. Visitors can explore the exhibit with audio guides and marvel at the stunning details of the manuscript. Additionally, Trinity College's Long Room library, dating back to the 18th century, holds 200,000 of the oldest books, including a display dedicated to Bram Stoker's Dracula.
