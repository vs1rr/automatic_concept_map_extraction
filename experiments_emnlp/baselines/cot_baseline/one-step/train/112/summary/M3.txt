The Book of Kells is a renowned example of Celtic and Irish art, serving as a unique copy of the New Testament of the Bible. Despite being a national symbol of Ireland, its exact origins remain a subject of debate. Created by Columban monks, the Book of Kells is made from vellum and features intricate illuminations using a wide variety of colors sourced from different materials. The book, believed to have originated in Iona and later moved to Kells and Dublin, is now displayed at Trinity College Library. The elaborate artwork in the Book of Kells showcases the meticulous craftsmanship and defines Celtic art.
