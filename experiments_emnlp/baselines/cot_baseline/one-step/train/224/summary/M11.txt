Stephen Hill, an African American slave, escaped from his owner in the Gold Rush era, leading to a legal battle over his freedom. Despite California being a free state, laws allowed for the capture of fugitive slaves. The state's admission to the Union as a free state in 1849 was a pivotal moment in the national debate over slavery. However, the decision was based on economic interests rather than racial equality, as African Americans and Native Americans were denied civil rights in California.
