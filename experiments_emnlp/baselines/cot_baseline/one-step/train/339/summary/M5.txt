James Madison's report discusses the Virginia Resolutions of 1798 concerning the Alien and Sedition Laws. The report emphasizes the importance of maintaining and defending the Constitution of the United States and the states, expressing warm attachment to the Union and pledging to oppose any violations of its principles. It delves into the powers of the federal government, the role of the states as parties to the Constitution, and the right of states to interpose in case of dangerous breaches of the Constitution. The report argues that the states, as sovereign parties to the constitutional compact, have the ultimate authority to judge violations of the Constitution, even by the judiciary. It concludes by highlighting the necessity of upholding fundamental principles and the sovereignty of the people over constitutions.
