Ptolemy I Soter, a Macedonian general under Alexander the Great, became ruler of Egypt and founded a dynasty that lasted for three centuries. He assimilated to Egyptian culture, assumed the title of pharaoh, and played a key role in the wars among Alexander's successors. Ptolemy was known for his shrewdness, patronage of letters, and founding the Great Library of Alexandria. He also sponsored the mathematician Euclid. Ptolemy's life and achievements have been portrayed in various fictional works.
