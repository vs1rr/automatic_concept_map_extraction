Constantinople, also known as Byzantium, New Rome, and Istanbul, was the capital of the Eastern Roman Empire after the fall of Rome. Established by Emperor Constantine in the early 4th century, the city was strategically located on a promontory with natural defenses and man-made fortifications, including the famous Theodosian walls. Constantinople boasted a valuable harbor and was surrounded by water on three sides. The city's geography included 7 hills similar to Rome. Various forums were built in Constantinople before the fall of Rome, showcasing its importance as a political and cultural center.
