The fall of Constantinople in 1453 marked a significant turning point in history, showcasing the power of gunpowder in warfare and the end of traditional fortress defenses. Despite the tragic events, the Muslim conquest led to the preservation of eastern Christendom's doctrinal integrity. The fall of Constantinople also triggered various geopolitical shifts, including the flight of Greek scholars to Italy, contributing to the Renaissance, and the emergence of power struggles between empires in southeastern Europe. The repercussions of this event are still felt today, influencing relationships between nations and shaping the course of history in the region.
