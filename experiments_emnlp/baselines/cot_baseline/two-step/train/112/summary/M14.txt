The Book of Kells, housed at Trinity College in Dublin, is a renowned illuminated manuscript created by Celtic monks around 800 AD. It contains the four Gospels of the New Testament and features intricate illustrations of humans, animals, and mythical creatures, all infused with Christian symbolism. The manuscript, written on calf vellum with iron gall ink and vibrant imported colors, attracts over 500,000 visitors annually. The exhibit offers a self-guided tour with illuminated replicas and a stunning library, including the Long Room with 200,000 ancient books and a display on Bram Stoker's Dracula.
