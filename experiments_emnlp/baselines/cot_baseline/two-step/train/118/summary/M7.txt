The Brougham Roman fort (Brocavum) and civil settlement, along with Brougham Castle, are designated as a scheduled monument of national importance. The fort and castle display significant historical and archaeological value, with well-preserved remains offering insights into Roman and medieval military occupation in northern England. The site has undergone limited excavations, revealing buried archaeological features and potential for further discoveries. The history of the fort and castle spans from Roman times to the medieval period, with notable developments and modifications over the centuries. The monument includes the fort, associated vicus, and the castle, each contributing to our understanding of the region's military and settlement history.
