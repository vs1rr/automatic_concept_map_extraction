Brougham Castle near Penrith is a captivating historical site with roots dating back to a Roman fort. Founded in the 13th century by the Norman family of Robert de Vieuxpont, the castle saw enhancements by Robert Clifford during the Anglo-Scottish wars. Over the years, it passed through generations until Lady Anne Clifford took charge in 1643, restoring the castle to its former glory. Despite later neglect, the castle remains a significant landmark, inspiring art and literature. Today, maintained by English Heritage, Brougham Castle offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history through exhibitions and guided tours.
