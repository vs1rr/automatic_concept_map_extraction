The text discusses the "federalism revolution" during the Rehnquist Court era, highlighting a resurgence of federalism and limited federal power. Cases like Gregory v. Ashcroft, New York v. United States, and others emphasize a narrow construction of federal power, with the Tenth Amendment serving as a key textual basis for this approach. James Madison's views on the division of powers between the federal and state governments are also referenced to support this interpretation.
