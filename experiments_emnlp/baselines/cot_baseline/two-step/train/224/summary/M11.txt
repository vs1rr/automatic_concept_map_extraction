Stephen Hill, an African American slave, escaped from his owner in the Gold Rush era, leading to a legal battle over his freedom. Despite California being a free state, laws allowed for the capture and return of fugitive slaves. The state's admission to the Union as a free state in 1849 was a pivotal moment in the national debate over slavery. However, the decision was based on economic interests rather than a commitment to racial equality, as African Americans and Native Americans were denied civil rights in California.
