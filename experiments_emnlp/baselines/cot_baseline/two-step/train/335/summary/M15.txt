The Pendle witches were a group of twelve people accused of witchcraft in the 17th century, with only one being found not guilty. The trials were well-documented and became a famous legend. The background to the events involved rival families and a society fascinated by witchcraft. King James I's interest in witchcraft influenced the fear and prosecution of witches. The trials in Lancashire reflected a time of unrest and unease, leading to the sentencing of the Pendle witches. The accusations stemmed from a feud between families and escalated due to general fear of witchcraft. The trials resulted in convictions and executions, making it one of the most notorious witch trials of the time.
