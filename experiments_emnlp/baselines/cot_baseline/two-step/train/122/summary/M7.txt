Bruce Castle Museum, Archives, and Park in Tottenham, London, is a Grade 1 listed building with a rich history dating back to the 16th century. Originally owned by Sir William Compton, it was later modified by notable figures such as Lord Coleraine and Rowland Hill. The building opened as a museum in 1906, showcasing the history of the London Borough of Haringey and housing its archives. The park, which opened in 1892, is Haringey's oldest park and has been enjoyed by generations of residents. Despite changes over the years, including the loss of many stately trees, the park remains a popular spot for various activities.
