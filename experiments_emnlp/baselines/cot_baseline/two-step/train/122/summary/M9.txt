Sir William Compton, a prominent figure in Tudor England, was a close friend of King Henry VIII and held various important positions in the royal court. He was involved in a scandal with Anne Stafford, leading to accusations of adultery. Compton's will suggested his guilt, despite his protests of innocence. He was also known for his involvement in arranging marriages and conflicts with Cardinal Wolsey. Compton passed away in 1528 due to the sweating sickness, leaving behind a legacy that included properties and estates for his descendants.
