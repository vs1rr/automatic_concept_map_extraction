Bruce Castle Museum is a 16th-century manor house in London, operated by Haringey Council as a local history museum. The museum offers exhibits on social history, local landscape, and works by local artists. The castle has a rich history dating back to the 13th century and is known for its connection to the Bruce family. The museum also features displays on the history of the Post Office and the life of Rowland Hill, as well as collections of early photos of the area and artwork depicting Tottenham through time. The museum is free to enter and is easily accessible by public transportation.
