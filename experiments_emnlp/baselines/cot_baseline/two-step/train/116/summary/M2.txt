Bramshill House, a Jacobean mansion in Hampshire, England, has a rich history dating back to Saxon times. It was rebuilt in the 14th century by Thomas Foxley and later completed by Edward Zouche in 1612. The property has seen various owners, including the Cope family and Lord Brocket. After World War II, it was home to the exiled King Michael and Queen Ana of Romania. The house also has a cricket ground that hosted first-class matches in the 19th century.
