The text discusses the significant role played by Edward I's Welsh Castles in the period 1297-1413. It explores the various functions of these castles, including their socio-economic, administrative, military, and propagandist roles. The text suggests that while the castles were important for enforcing English rule in Wales, their socio-economic and propaganda functions may have been more crucial than their military significance. The castles were seen as symbols of power and authority, attracting settlers and reinforcing English control over Wales. Despite their immense cost and grandeur, the castles were effective in maintaining English supremacy and deterring Welsh uprisings, ultimately contributing to the lasting English presence in Wales.
