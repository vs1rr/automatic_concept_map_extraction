The text discusses the historical significance and functions of castles in medieval England. It highlights how castles served both offensive and defensive military roles, as well as social and administrative functions for the lord of the land. The evolution of castle construction from wood to stone for better defense against attacks is also detailed. Additionally, the text explains how advancements in weaponry, particularly gunpowder, led to the decline of traditional castles. Despite their diminished military role, castles remain popular tourist attractions today.
