The text discusses the complexities of heritage tourism at Borobudur Temple in Indonesia, focusing on the impact of tourism on local communities and the construction of Indonesian identity. It highlights the challenges faced by local vendors, the experiences of international and domestic tourists, and the conflicting interests of various stakeholders involved in the management and development of the site. The text emphasizes the need for better dialogue and collaboration between government officials, planners, and local residents to ensure the sustainable development of Borobudur Temple and to address the issues arising from tourism.
