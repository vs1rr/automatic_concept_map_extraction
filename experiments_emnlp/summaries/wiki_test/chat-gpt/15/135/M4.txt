Sir Arthur Conan Doyle was a British author best known for creating the detective Sherlock Holmes.
Born in 1859 in Edinburgh, Scotland, he studied medicine at the University of Edinburgh and was influenced by his professor, Dr. Joseph Bell, who inspired the character of Holmes.
Conan Doyle wrote numerous Sherlock Holmes stories, but also delved into historical fiction, nonfiction, and spiritualism.
He married twice and had five children.
Despite his success with Sherlock Holmes, Conan Doyle believed his other works deserved more recognition.
He was knighted for his work during the Boer War and became a prominent advocate for spiritualism.
Conan Doyle passed away in 1930, and his funeral was celebrated by his family and the spiritualist community.
