President William McKinley was assassinated by Leon Czolgosz in 1901 at the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo.
Czolgosz confessed to the murder, stating he did it because he believed in equality of service.
McKinley died on September 14, 1901, and Czolgosz was sentenced to death by electric chair and executed on October 29, 1901.
The assassination fueled fears of anarchists in the United States, leading to legislation targeting anarchists and immigrants.
The Erie County Bar Association provided defense attorneys for Czolgosz, who was quickly convicted and sentenced.
