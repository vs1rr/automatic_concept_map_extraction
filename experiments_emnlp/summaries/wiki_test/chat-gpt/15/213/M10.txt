President William McKinley was assassinated by anarchist Leon Czolgosz at the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo, New York on September 6, 1901.
Czolgosz shot McKinley twice with a revolver concealed in a handkerchief during a public reception, causing serious internal injuries.
Despite initial improvement, McKinley died on September 14.
Vice President Theodore Roosevelt succeeded him as President.
Czolgosz was found guilty of murder
