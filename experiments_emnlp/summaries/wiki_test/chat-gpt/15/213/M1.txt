William McKinley was the 25th President of the United States, serving from 1897 until his assassination in 1901.
He led the nation to victory in the Spanish-American War and raised protective tariffs to promote American industry.
McKinley's presidency was marked by economic prosperity and a focus on foreign policy, particularly in relation to Cuba and the Philippines.
He was known for his strong stance against trusts and his support for the highest tariff in history.
McKinley won a second term in 1900 but was tragically assassinated in 1901.
