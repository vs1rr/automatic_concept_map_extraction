In 1981, Irish republican prisoners embarked on a hunger strike to protest the removal of Political Prisoner Status, which led to the deaths of ten men, including Bobby Sands.
The strike attracted international attention and public support, with Sands even being elected to the British Parliament.
Despite the prisoners' sacrifices, the British government initially refused to meet their demands, leading to the deaths of the hunger strikers.
Eventually, the strike was called off, and the prisoners were granted some concessions, but the British government still refused to officially recognize them as political prisoners.
The hunger strike brought the Irish struggle for freedom to the forefront of world attention and paved the way for Sinn Fein's entry into politics and eventual peace in Ireland.
