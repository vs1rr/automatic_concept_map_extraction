The text discusses the mural of the ten prisoners who died in the 1981 hunger strike in Belfast, highlighting the differing interpretations of the strike's significance.
It explores the hidden history of mass and class struggle during the hunger strike, emphasizing the importance of understanding this context.
The text argues that the hunger strike was a pivotal moment in the struggle against British imperialism, but ultimately led to Sinn Fein's shift towards accommodation with imperialism through the Good Friday agreement.
It suggests that a new resistance must be built by opposing the agreement, supporting democracy, and rejecting alliances with Irish capital.
The text calls for a renewed focus on self-organization of the masses, appealing to the working class to resist imperialism and capitalism.
