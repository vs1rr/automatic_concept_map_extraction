In 1981, Bernie Sanders, then Mayor of Burlington, made a direct appeal to Margaret Thatcher on behalf of IRA hunger strikers.
The documents in the Vermont archive reveal Sanders' involvement in advocating for the prisoners' rights during the height of the troubles in Northern Ireland.
Sanders expressed concern over the treatment of Irish prisoners and urged Thatcher to change her policy.
The Irish Republican movement had a strong influence in Burlington during the 1980s, and Sanders was actively involved in supporting their cause.
Sanders' actions were part of a larger trend of pro-Irish activism in the United States, where those of Irish descent held political influence.
