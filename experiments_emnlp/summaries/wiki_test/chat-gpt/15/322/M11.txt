The Crow Dog Case of 1883 involved a Brule Sioux man named Crow Dog who killed Spotted Tail, leading to a legal battle over jurisdiction on reservation land.
The U.S. Supreme Court ruled that unless Congress authorized it, the government did not have jurisdiction over crimes committed by one Indian against another on reservations.
