Ex Parte Crow Dog was a case in which the U.S. Supreme Court determined that the 1st Judicial District Court of Dakota had jurisdiction over offenses committed within the Sioux reservation.
The Court also discussed the definition of "Indian Country" and the extension of U.S. legislation over Indian territory through treaties.
The Court emphasized that statutes should not be repealed by implication and that clear expressions of Congressional intent are necessary for repeals.
The case highlighted the importance of respecting tribal sovereignty and treaty agreements.
