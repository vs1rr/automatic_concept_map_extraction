The article discusses the "federalism revolution" during the Rehnquist Court era, highlighting cases that emphasized a narrow construction of federal power and a resurgence of federalism.
The Tenth Amendment played a key role in this shift, with Justice O'Connor emphasizing the limited powers of the federal government and the substantial authority retained by the states.
James Madison's views on the division of powers between the federal and state governments are also referenced, with an emphasis on the states having authority over matters concerning the lives, liberties, and properties of the people.
Overall, the article explores the transformation of the Tenth Amendment and its impact on the balance of power between the federal government and the states during the Rehnquist Court era.
