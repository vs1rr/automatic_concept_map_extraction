Brougham Castle in Cumbria was built in 1214 by Robert de Vieuxpont on the site of a Roman fort.
The castle's great tower still stands, surrounded by a stone curtain wall built by Robert Clifford in the 1300s.
It played a significant role in the Anglo-Scottish wars and the Wars of the Roses.
Lady Anne Clifford once lived there.
