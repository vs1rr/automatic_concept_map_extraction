The Brougham Roman fort (Brocavum) and civil settlement, along with Brougham Castle, are scheduled monuments of national importance.
The fort was constructed during the Roman occupation and was later accompanied by a vicus or civilian settlement.
Brougham Castle was built in the early 13th century and underwent various modifications over the centuries.
The site has been subject to limited archaeological excavations, revealing well-preserved remains of the Roman and medieval periods.
The monument is significant for its historical and archaeological value, providing insights into the military occupation and settlement of northern England.
The boundaries of the scheduled areas encompass the fort, castle, and associated vicus, ensuring their protection and preservation for future generations.
