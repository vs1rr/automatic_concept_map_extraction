Tomm Moore, the director of the independent animated film "The Secret of Kells," discusses the inspiration behind the film, which draws from Irish tradition and Celtic aesthetics.
Moore talks about the various artistic influences that shaped the look of the film, including medieval art, comics, and Eastern European animation.
He also touches on the importance of storytelling in the film and the value of hand-drawn animation in a world dominated by computer-generated animation.
Moore shares insights into the development of the character Aisling and the blending of old and new faiths in the film.
He also discusses the impact of the blogosphere on the movie business and the special features included in the DVD release of "The Secret of Kells."
Moore hints at his next project, "Song of the Sea," which explores Irish folklore and the disappearing creatures of legend.
Moore offers words of advice for aspiring animators, emphasizing patience and persistence.
