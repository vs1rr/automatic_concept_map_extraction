The CSI effect refers to the exaggerated portrayal of forensic science on TV shows, leading to unrealistic public perceptions.
It influences public perception by shaping views on crime scene investigation, technology, evidence collection, and the speed of scientific outcomes.
This is important because public expectations can impact jury trials, potentially leading to wrongful acquittals.
The effect has caused jurors to expect more forensic evidence than necessary and to have unwarranted confidence in DNA evidence.
As a result, prosecutors have had to adjust their approach to presenting cases, while defense attorneys have new ways to create doubt in jurors' minds.
The CSI effect has also led to changes in jury selection, with a focus on potential jurors' TV viewing habits.
