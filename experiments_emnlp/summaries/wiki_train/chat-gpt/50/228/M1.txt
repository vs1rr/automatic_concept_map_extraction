The Convention of 1833, held on April 1, was more aggressive than the previous one, with only a quarter of the delegates attending both.
William H. Wharton presided over the convention, with Sam Houston also in attendance.
The main agenda remained the same, with the addition of drafting a constitution to be submitted to the Mexican congress.
Stephen F. Austin was chosen to present the petitions to the Mexican government, initially having positive meetings but eventually being imprisoned without charges in early 1834.
