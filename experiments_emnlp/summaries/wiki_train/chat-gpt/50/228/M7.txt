The annual Texana Grand Format Auction held by Heritage Auctions in Dallas featured rare historical documents related to Texas history, with a scarce promissory note signed by Jim Bowie selling for $62,500.
Bowie was a key figure in the Texas Revolution and died at the Battle of the Alamo.
The auction also included items such as a historic broadside by Sam Houston, a map of Texas by Charles W. Pressler, and letters signed by fallen heroes of the Alamo like Davy Crockett and William Barret Travis.
Other notable items sold included a land grant signed by Stephen F. Austin and a document signed by Mathew Caldwell, known as Texas' Paul Revere.
The auction realized a total of $587,701, with nearly 400 bidders participating.
The auction director, Sandra Palomino, highlighted the significance of these items in shaping the history of Texas and noted that the auction provided a unique opportunity for collectors to own pieces of this history.
The auction also featured items like a surety bond signed by Crockett, a letter signed by Benjamin McCulloch with an endorsement by James Buchanan, and a pine mantle used in a stagecoach stop on the Texas frontier.
Overall, the auction showcased a range of rare and valuable items related to Texas history, attracting interest from collectors and history enthusiasts alike.
