In episode 8 of "Manhattan," the focus shifts to the challenges faced by the scientists in developing a plutonium bomb.
Physicist Charlie Isaacs discovers that the Thin Man project, which relies on plutonium-239, will not work due to impurities in the plutonium produced at the X-10 Reactor.
This leads him to realize the urgency of developing an alternative method for a plutonium bomb.
The episode also highlights the moral dilemma faced by the scientists, particularly in light of the escalating persecution of Jews in Europe by the Nazis.
The fear of the Nazis obtaining the bomb first is a driving force behind the scientists' efforts.
The episode also sheds light on the contributions of the British Mission to the Manhattan Project, emphasizing the importance of their involvement in the project's success.
Additionally, the role of women at Los Alamos is explored, showcasing the diverse contributions made by female scientists and workers during the project.
The episode sets the stage for further developments as the scientists continue to work towards designing a functional bomb and addressing personal and ethical challenges.
