The Late Period of Ancient Egypt was a time of native Egyptian rulers between the Third Intermediate Period and the Persian conquests.
It is often seen as a decline in power for Egypt.
The Twenty-Sixth Dynasty, also known as the Saite Period, was followed by the First Persian Occupation during the Twenty-Seventh Dynasty.
The Twenty-Eighth Dynasty was led by a single king who rebelled against the Persians, leaving no monuments.
The Twenty-Ninth Dynasty ruled from Mendes, while the Thirtieth Dynasty adopted the art style of the Twenty-Sixth Dynasty before being re-occupied by the Persians.
