On September 7, 1822, Dom Pedro I declared Brazil's independence from Portugal, marking the end of colonial rule.
Portugal had previously neglected Brazil, focusing on other territories for profit.
The Portuguese Crown controlled Brazil through resource extraction and strict regulations, limiting development.
When Napoleon invaded Portugal in 1808, the royal court moved to Rio de Janeiro, sparking growth and cultural flourishing in Brazil.
After Napoleon's defeat, the Portuguese Crown attempted to regain control, leading to tensions with Brazilian elites.
In 1822, Pedro I proclaimed independence, leading to a peaceful break from Portugal.
Despite the heroic portrayal of the event, Pedro I was reportedly ill at the time.
