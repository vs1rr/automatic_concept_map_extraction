The Brazilian Empire existed from 1822 to 1889 and was led by Dom Pedro I and his son, Dom Pedro II.
Brazil was politically stable and had a powerful army, winning wars and expanding its borders.
It intervened in conflicts in Uruguay and Argentina, and also fought in the War of the Triple Alliance.
The empire was known for its political stability, economic growth, and respect for civil rights, although women and slaves had limited rights.
Dom Pedro I abdicated in 1831, and his son, Dom Pedro II, became emperor.
Under his rule, Brazil experienced peace and stability, becoming an emerging international power.
The empire abolished slavery in 1888.
