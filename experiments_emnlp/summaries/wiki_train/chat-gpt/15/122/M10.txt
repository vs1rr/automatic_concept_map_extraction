Bruce Castle Museum is a 16th-century manor house in London operated by Haringey Council as a local history museum.
The museum offers exhibits on social history, local landscape, and works by local artists.
The castle is named after the Bruce family, who owned parts of the manor of Tottenham from the 13th century.
The house has a round tower of red brick, thought to be one of the earliest uses of brick for an English house.
The museum covers the history of the London Borough of Haringey, including the Post Office and the life of Rowland Hill.
