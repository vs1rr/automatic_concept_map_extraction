Bruce Castle, also known as Lordship House, is a manor house in Tottenham, London with a rich history and even a resident ghost.
Despite its grand name, it is believed to have no connection to the Bruce family.
The house has undergone numerous remodels and changes in ownership throughout the centuries.
It was converted into a school in the 19th century under headmaster Sir Rowland Hill, who later became head of the General Post Office.
The school was known for its progressive curriculum and teaching methods.
After the school closed, the house became a museum in 1906.
