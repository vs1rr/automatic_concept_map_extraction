Constantine the Great was a Roman Emperor who rebuilt Byzantium and renamed it Nova Roma.
He defeated Maxentius at the Battle of Milvian Bridge in 312 and became Western Augustus until his death in 337.
Constantine was the first Roman Emperor to embrace Christianity and issued the Edict of Milan in 313, legalizing Christianity in the Roman Empire.
Justinian, who ruled from 527 to 565, reunited the Roman Empire and created the Justinian's Code.
After his death, the empire began to decline due to invasions.
The Byzantine Empire, influenced by Greek and Roman roots, reflected outside influences from trade.
Byzantine emperors held more power than western emperors and were considered the head of the church.
