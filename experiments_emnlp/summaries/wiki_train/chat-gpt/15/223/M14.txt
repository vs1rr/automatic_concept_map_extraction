The Macedonian Dynasty, which began in 867 CE, oversaw the recovery and expansion of the Byzantine Empire, leading to a period known as the Byzantine Renaissance.
This era saw a flourishing of art, literature, science, and philosophy, influenced by the classical Greco-Roman heritage.
The dynasty also expanded the empire by conquering territories such as Crete, Cyprus, and Syria.
