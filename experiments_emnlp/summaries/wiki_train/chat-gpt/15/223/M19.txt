The fall of Constantinople on May 29th, 1453, marked a significant turning point in history as the Ottoman forces overran the city, ending the era of immobile, iron-clad soldiers defending stone fortresses.
The fall of Constantinople had far-reaching consequences, including the preservation of the doctrinal integrity of eastern Christendom and the shift of political capital of eastern Orthodoxy to Russia.
The fall of Constantinople also led to the emergence of new power dynamics in southeastern Europe, with the Ottoman Empire engaging in conflicts with Austria-Hungary and Russia.
