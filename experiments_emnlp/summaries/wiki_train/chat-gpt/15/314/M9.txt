The CSI Effect refers to the influence of crime television shows on jurors, leading them to demand more forensic evidence in trials.
This phenomenon has raised the burden of proof for prosecutors and increased expectations for forensic science capabilities.
Jurors now often request unnecessary tests and procedures, based on what they have seen on TV.
This poses challenges for both prosecution and defense in legal proceedings.
While forensic evidence is important, it is not always necessary for a successful case.
It is crucial to consider all evidence in context when reaching a verdict.
