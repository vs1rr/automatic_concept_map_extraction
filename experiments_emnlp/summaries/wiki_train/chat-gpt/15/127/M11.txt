The text discusses the history and functions of castles in medieval England.
Castles served both offensive and defensive military purposes, as well as symbolizing power and serving as important social centers.
Initially built from earth and timber, castles were later replaced by stone for better defense against attacks.
Various improvements in castle defenses, such as arrow-slits and multiple walls, were made to counter raids by Vikings and other attackers.
The introduction of gunpowder eventually led to the decline of traditional castles, as artillery became powerful enough to break through stone walls.
Despite their diminished military role, remaining castles continue to attract visitors interested in experiencing their historical significance.
