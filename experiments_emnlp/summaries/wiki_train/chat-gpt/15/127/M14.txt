Concentric castles are fortified structures with two or more curtain walls, where the outer wall is lower than the inner wall.
They were developed in response to advances in siege technology during the Crusades.
The design allowed for better defense against siege engines and provided flanking fire from crossbows.
Concentric castles were expensive to build and were mainly constructed by powerful military orders or kings.
The layout suited the requirements of military orders, resembling a monastery and housing a large garrison.
