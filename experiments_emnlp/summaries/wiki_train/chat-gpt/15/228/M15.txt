The presentation discusses the tension between Mexico and Texas from 1823-1833.
When the Federalists were in power in Mexico City, Texas colonists were able to live undisturbed, creating their own schools, newspapers, and bringing in slaves.
However, when the Centralists came to power in 1829, they put an end to the Texans' independent decision-making.
The presentation highlights events such as the Fredonian Rebellion, the Mier y Teran investigation, the Law of April 6, 1830, the conflict at Anahuac, the Turtle Bayou Resolutions, the Battle of Velasco, and the Conventions of 1832 and 1833.
