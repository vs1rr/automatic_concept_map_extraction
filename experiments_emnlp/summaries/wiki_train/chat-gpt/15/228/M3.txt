Sam Houston was a prominent figure in Texas history, known for his leadership during the Texas Revolution and as the first elected president of the Republic of Texas.
Born in Tennessee, Houston had a varied career that included serving in the United States Army, Congress, and as governor of Tennessee.
He played a crucial role in the Texas Revolution, leading the Texas army to victory at the Battle of San Jacinto, where they defeated the Mexican army and captured General Santa Anna.
Houston served as president of the Republic of Texas and later as a U.S. Senator.
Despite his opposition to secession, he was removed from office for refusing to swear loyalty to the Confederacy.
He died in Huntsville, Texas in 1863.
