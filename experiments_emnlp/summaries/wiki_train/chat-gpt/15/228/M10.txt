The State of Coahuila y Tejas, Estados Unidos Mexicanos, had a constitutional tradition that began under the Mexican Constitution of 1824.
The constitution was influenced by the United States and Spanish constitutions, with elements such as the tripartite division of governmental powers and the establishment of Catholicism as the state religion.
The state legislature in Saltillo published a state constitution in 1827, which included provisions for separate executive, legislative, and judicial authority.
The constitution also incorporated Spanish legal traditions, such as the community property system and homestead exemption from bankruptcy.
