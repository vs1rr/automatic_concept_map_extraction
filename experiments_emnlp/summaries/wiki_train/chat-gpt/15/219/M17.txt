In episode 8 of "Manhattan," the focus shifts to the challenges faced by scientists in developing a plutonium bomb.
Physicist Charlie Isaacs discovers that the Thin Man project is doomed due to impurities in the plutonium produced at the X-10 Reactor.
As the scientists grapple with the moral implications of their work, Abby learns about the Nazi persecution of Jews in Europe, prompting Charlie to realize the urgency of developing the bomb before the Nazis.
The episode also highlights the contributions of the British Mission to the Manhattan Project and the role of women at Los Alamos, including their struggles for professional recognition.
As tensions rise and doubts about the implosion method surface, the scientists face a serious quandary in designing a viable bomb.
The episode sets the stage for further developments as the race for the bomb intensifies and moral dilemmas come to the forefront.
