The Alien & Sedition Acts of 1798 were passed amidst concerns about their constitutionality, particularly regarding freedom of speech and the press.
James Madison, a key figure in the creation of the Constitution and Bill of Rights, criticized the Sedition Act as unconstitutional, arguing that the First Amendment guaranteed absolute freedom against the federal government.
Madison's criticisms were rooted in the fear that the Sedition Act could be used similarly to the Seditious Libel law in England.
The Acts were passed in response to tensions with France, with Congress supporting President John Adams' concerns about the threat posed by France.
