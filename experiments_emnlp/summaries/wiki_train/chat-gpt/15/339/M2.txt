James Madison was born in 1751 in Virginia and had a strong education, eventually attending Princeton University.
He became involved in politics during the American Revolution and played a key role in the Constitutional Convention, helping to create the Virginia Plan.
Madison served in Congress and as Secretary of State before becoming the fourth President of the United States.
His presidency was marked by the War of 1812 against Great Britain.
After retiring, Madison continued to be involved in politics and passed away in 1836.
He was remembered as a key figure in American history and a pillar of light for future patriots.
