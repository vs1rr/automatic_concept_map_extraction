Brougham Castle near Penrith, founded in the early 13th century on the site of a Roman fort, is a fascinating place to explore with nearly two thousand years of history.
Originally built by the Norman family of Robert de Vieuxpont, the castle passed to the Clifford family in 1268.
The castle was strengthened during the Anglo-Scottish wars, hosting Edward 1 in 1300.
