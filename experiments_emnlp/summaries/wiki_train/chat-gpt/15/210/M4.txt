Ancient Egypt was a powerful civilization along the Nile River for over 3000 years.
It was divided into three major kingdoms and had rich culture in government, religion, arts, and writing.
The Pharaohs were buried in pyramids with treasure for the afterlife.
The empire weakened and was conquered by other civilizations, including the Romans.
Egyptians wore makeup, used moldy bread for infections, and were advanced in science and math.
They invented writing, ink, paper, and toothpaste.
Cats were sacred, and the Bible mentions Egypt.
