The Borobudur Temple Compounds in central Java is a famous Buddhist temple dating back to the 8th and 9th centuries.
It consists of a pyramidal base with terraces, circular platforms, and a monumental stupa, all decorated with fine reliefs.
The temple represents the Buddhist concept of the Universe and the stages to attain Nirvana.
The site includes two smaller temples, Mendut and Pawon, and is considered an outstanding example of Indonesian art and architecture.
The temple was used for Buddhist worship until the 15th century when it was abandoned, but has since been restored and is now a Buddhist archaeological site.
The property faces threats from development, tourism, and natural disasters like volcanic eruptions.
The temple has been reconstructed using original materials and is protected under Indonesian cultural heritage laws.
Management of the site involves various government agencies and community development programs.
The property is monitored for deterioration and damage, with plans in place for long-term protection and conservation.
The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a significant cultural and historical landmark in Indonesia.
