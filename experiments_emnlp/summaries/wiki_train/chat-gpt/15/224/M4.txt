The California Gold Rush began in 1848 when gold was discovered in the American River, leading to a massive influx of immigrants known as "'49ers" to California.
The population of California grew rapidly, leading to its incorporation as the 31st state in 1850.
The discovery of gold also led to global emigration, with fortune-seekers coming from China, Germany, Chile, Mexico, Ireland, Turkey, and France.
