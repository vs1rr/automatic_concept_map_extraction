The California Gold Country, located between the Central Valley and the High Sierra, is rich in history and natural beauty.
Known for its mineral deposits and gold mines, the region is home to Coloma, where gold was first discovered in California.
The area features historic downtowns, award-winning wineries, and picturesque bed and breakfasts.
The Empire Mine in Grass Valley was the oldest, largest, and richest gold mine in California, producing 5.8 million ounces of gold.
The region offers recreational opportunities such as white water rafting, hiking, mountain biking, boating, and fishing.
The Gold Country was home to Native American tribes and played a significant role in the California Gold Rush, attracting people from around the world.
