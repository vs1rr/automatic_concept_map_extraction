The Borobudur Temple Compounds in central Java is a famous Buddhist temple dating back to the 8th and 9th centuries.
It consists of a pyramidal base with five square terraces, a trunk with three circular platforms, and a monumental stupa at the top.
The walls are adorned with intricate low reliefs, and there are 72 openwork stupas around the circular platforms, each containing a Buddha statue.
The temple represents the Buddhist concept of the universe divided into three spheres.
It is a blend of ancestor worship and the pursuit of Nirvana.
The temple, along with two smaller temples nearby, represents phases in the attainment of Nirvana.
The site was used as a Buddhist temple until the 15th century when it was abandoned.
It was rediscovered in the 19th century and restored in the 20th century.
The temple is considered a masterpiece of Buddhist architecture and monumental arts, exerting influence on Indonesian art and architecture.
The property faces threats from development, tourism, deterioration of building stone, unsupervised visitors, and volcanic eruptions.
The temple has been reconstructed using original materials and is protected under Indonesian cultural heritage laws.
The management of the property involves various government bodies and includes conservation efforts, monitoring programs, community development initiatives, and plans for risk preparedness.
The temple is a popular tourist destination and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
