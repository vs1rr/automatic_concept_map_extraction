Sir William Compton, a Knight born in England around 1475-1492, was a close friend of King Henry VIII.
He was married twice, first to Warburga Brereton and then to Elizabeth Stonor.
Compton was involved in a scandal with Anne Stafford, the sister of the Duke of Buckingham, which led to a falling out between Buckingham and the King.
Compton was accused of adultery with Anne in 1527, but he protested his innocence.
He built a chantry and allocated land profits for her use, indicating his genuine affection for her.
Compton received Bruce Castle in 1514 and may have built a Tudor house on the site.
The Round Tower at the castle was possibly used for falconry.
Compton arranged a marriage for his daughter, Catherine, but she died before it could take place.
Compton served as Groom of the Stole to the King, causing tension with Cardinal Wolsey.
He fell ill and died in 1528 during an outbreak of the sweating sickness.
After his death, George Throckmorton sought to inherit Compton's positions and properties.
Compton's son Peter inherited Wooburn Manor, and his grandson Henry started Castle Ashley.
Sir William Compton was a prominent figure in the court of Henry VIII, known for his close relationship with the King and involvement in various scandals and political intrigues.
