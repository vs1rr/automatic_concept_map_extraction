The text discusses the Anglo-American Colonization Gallery Constitutional Alcalde of the Jurisdiction of Austin held in San Felipe de Austin in 1832.
The Convention adopted resolutions to be presented to the Mexican Congress, including organizing a militia and committees.
Although the resolutions were not presented to Congress, some were upheld.
Edward Burleson was confirmed as a lieutenant colonel in a provisional militia.
In 1833, another convention in San Felipe de Austin petitioned for the separation of Coahuila and Texas into two states and prepared a constitution for Texas.
A committee was appointed to write a memorial to the Mexican government, which Stephen F. Austin presented in Mexico City.
The text includes certificates and memorials related to these events.
