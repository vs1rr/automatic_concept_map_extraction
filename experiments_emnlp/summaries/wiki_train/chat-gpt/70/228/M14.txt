In the early 1800s, Mexico declared independence from Spain, leading to the establishment of a federal republic.
Moses Austin was granted permission to establish a colony in Texas, which was continued by his son Stephen F. Austin.
Tensions between Texan settlers and the Mexican government escalated, leading to the outbreak of the Texas Revolution in 1835.
Battles such as Gonzales, Goliad, and the Grass Fight took place, with Texan forces gaining momentum.
The Alamo fell in March 1836, resulting in the deaths of key figures like William B. Travis, Davy Crockett, and Jim Bowie.
The Battle of San Jacinto in April 1836 saw Sam Houston's Texan army defeat Santa Anna's Mexican forces, securing Texas independence.
Treaties of Velasco were signed, solidifying the end of the Texas Revolution.
