Brougham Castle in Cumbria was built in 1214 by Robert de Vieuxpont on the site of a Roman fort.
The castle's great tower still stands, although later buildings were destroyed by fire in 1521.
The keep and service buildings were originally surrounded by a timber palisade, which was later replaced by a stone curtain wall by Robert Clifford around 1300.
The castle played a significant role in the Anglo-Scottish wars and the Wars of the Roses.
Over time, Brougham Castle's military importance declined and it fell into periods of neglect.
