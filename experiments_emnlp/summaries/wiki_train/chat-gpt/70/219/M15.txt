During World War 2, British scientists joined the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos to help develop the atomic bomb.
This book highlights their contributions, often overlooked, that were vital to the project's completion.
The collaboration between British, American, and Canadian scientists had a profound impact on shaping the nuclear programs of the post-War world, including those of the United States, Great Britain, and the USSR.
The story of these scientists and their role in the Manhattan Project is told in this book, shedding light on their important but often forgotten part in the race to beat the Germans to the secret of the atomic bomb.
