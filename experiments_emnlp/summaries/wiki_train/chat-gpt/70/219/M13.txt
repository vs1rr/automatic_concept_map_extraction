Mark Oliphant was a physicist born in Australia in 1901, who became a key figure in the development of the atom bomb during World War II.
Despite his initial involvement in the project, Oliphant later became a vocal advocate for the peaceful use of atomic energy and spoke out against the use of nuclear weapons.
He was appalled by the devastation caused by the bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki and dedicated his later years to promoting disarmament and opposing nuclear testing.
Oliphant's contributions to science and his humanitarian efforts were recognized through various honors and awards, including a knighthood.
He passed away in 2000, leaving behind a legacy of scientific achievement and advocacy for peace.
