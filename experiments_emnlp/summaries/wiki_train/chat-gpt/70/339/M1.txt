The article discusses the "federalism revolution" under the Rehnquist Court, which emphasized a limited construction of federal power and a resurgence of federalism.
Cases like Gregory v. Ashcroft, New York v. United States, and United States v. Lopez all focused on interpreting the Tenth Amendment to restrict federal interference in matters best left to the states.
Justice O'Connor highlighted the Constitution's creation of a Federal Government with limited powers, with the Tenth Amendment reserving substantial sovereign authority to the states.
James Madison's view that the federal government's powers are few and defined, while those of the states are numerous and indefinite, is also discussed.
Overall, the article emphasizes the importance of the Tenth Amendment in maintaining the balance of power between the federal government and the states, and how it has been used to limit federal interference in state matters.
