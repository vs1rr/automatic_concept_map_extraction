The Book of Kells is a renowned illuminated manuscript from medieval Europe, named after the Abbey of Kells where it was held for centuries.
Produced around 800 CE in Scotland and Ireland, it contains the four Gospels in Latin with expert calligraphy and decoration.
The Book of Kells is considered a masterpiece of western calligraphy and is celebrated for its rare colors and embellishments.
A set of three notebooks inspired by the Book of Kells pays homage to its artistry and is part of a line of exquisitely crafted gift products.
Published by Thames & Hudson, known for high-quality books on various subjects, the notebooks celebrate the most important surviving work of Celtic art and Ireland's greatest national treasure.
