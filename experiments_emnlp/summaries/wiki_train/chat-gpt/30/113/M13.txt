The reviewer describes their visit to Borobudur, the largest Buddhist monument in the world, located in Java, Indonesia.
They arrived early in the morning to witness a stunning sunrise and were impressed by the massive size and architectural marvel of the temple.
The temple, built in the 9th century, consists of stacked platforms decorated with bas-relief panels and Buddha statues.
The structure reflects a mix of Hindu and Buddhist influences, with depictions of daily life, Buddha's life, and Hindu figures.
The temple was abandoned in the 14th century and rediscovered in the 19th century.
It has suffered damage from natural disasters and terrorist attacks but remains a UNESCO World Heritage site.
The reviewer recommends visiting early in the morning to avoid the heat and crowds, and describes the experience as special and blessed.
They note the significance of the site for Buddhist pilgrims and the spiritual atmosphere felt by visitors, particularly during sunrise.
The reviewer also mentions the importance of preserving the temple's cultural heritage and the need for security due to threats from terrorist groups.
Overall, the reviewer had a positive experience at Borobudur and recommends it as a must-visit destination for those interested in history, culture, and spirituality.
