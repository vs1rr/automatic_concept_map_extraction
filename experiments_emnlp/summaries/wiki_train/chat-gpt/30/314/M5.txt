The CSI effect refers to the unrealistic public perception of forensic science's effectiveness and availability in the criminal justice system, influenced by crime scene television dramas like CSI.
These shows have raised juror expectations for forensic evidence, altered prosecutorial behavior, and portrayed forensic scientists as infallible.
While some argue that TV forensic dramas provide closure and justice that is elusive in the real world, others believe they give the public unrealistic expectations of forensic science capabilities.
However, studies show that people can distinguish between fact and fiction, understanding that TV shows are for entertainment rather than education.
The public does not necessarily expect real forensic science to match what is seen on TV, as evidenced by a juror dismissing sophisticated forensic techniques as "just CSI stuff."
