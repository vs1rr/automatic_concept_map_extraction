Medieval castles were fortified structures that offered protection to nobility and other classes of people, with some exceptions where they were used strictly for strategic positioning.
They originated in the 9th and 10th centuries in Europe, with nobles building them to control surrounding areas and protect their families and wealth.
The best protection was offered by castles owned by the king and other nobles, with urban castles also enforced by sheriffs to maintain peace.
The Normans were master castle builders who introduced many designs, with later medieval castle designs being more sophisticated and offering better defenses.


Castles were strategically located in areas such as mountain passes or near rivers to ensure the best chance of survival.
Concentric castles, with several outer walls, became popular in late medieval times due to their improved defenses.
Early medieval castles were easier and quicker to construct, with the Norman Motte and Bailey design being popular.
Famous medieval castles were mainly built during the Norman period of England's history.


Castle entertainment was important to keep the people working in the castle in good spirits.
Castle life was hard for lower-level staff, but the lord of the castle lived in luxury in the castle keep.
A large number of troops, including knights, guards, and archers, were needed to defend a medieval castle.
Castle sieges were a game of cat and mouse, with weapons such as battering rams and trebuchets being popular.


Medieval castles were built to protect wealth and resources, with the earliest fortifications originating in the Fertile Crescent and Egypt.
Castles were built according to the surrounding region and country they were protecting, with early forms of architecture used.
Castle parts such as arrow slits, battlements, curtain walls, the castle keep, gatehouses, and moats were common features in medieval castles.


England is home to many famous medieval castles, such as Warwick Castle, Windsor Castle, and the Tower of London.
European medieval castles first appeared in Normandy, France, with the Motte and Bailey design being popular.
Famous medieval castles in Europe include Chateau Gaillard in France, Cite de Carcassonne, Leeds Castle, and Windsor Castle.


Today, many medieval castles are used as tourist attractions, with guided tours and historical information available.
Restoration of castles may be required if they were damaged during wars, with new staircases, walking paths, and restored towers added.
Castles serve as reminders of the darker ages of war, with their interiors and exteriors bearing the marks of past conflicts.
