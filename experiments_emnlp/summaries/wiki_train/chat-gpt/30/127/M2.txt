Castles were initially made of wood, but in the 10th century, people began constructing them out of stone with walls up to 30 feet thick.
Some castles had moats and drawbridges for protection.
Castles were strategically built on high mountains, along rivers, lakes, hilltops, and near the sea.
They had holes in the walls for archers to shoot arrows and heavy iron doors for security.
Castles were expensive to maintain and only kings, lords, and noblemen could afford to live in them.
They were important for defense in a divided Europe, with villages around them providing food.
