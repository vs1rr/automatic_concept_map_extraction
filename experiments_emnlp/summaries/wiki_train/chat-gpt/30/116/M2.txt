Bramshill House is a Jacobean mansion in Hampshire, England, with a history dating back to Saxon times.
It has been the Police Staff College since 1955.
The house was rebuilt in the 14th century by Thomas Foxley, who used masons from Windsor.
In 1605, Edward Zouche bought the property and completed the current house in 1612.
The property changed hands several times before being occupied by the Cope family until 1935.
The chapel was demolished during the 18th century.
