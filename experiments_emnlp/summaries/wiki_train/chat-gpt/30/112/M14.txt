The Book of Kells, housed at Trinity College in Dublin, is a stunning illuminated manuscript created by Celtic monks around 800 AD.
It contains the four Gospels of the New Testament and is known for its intricate illustrations and ornate details.
The manuscript, written on calf vellum with iron gall ink and vibrant imported colors, attracts over 500,000 visitors annually.
The exhibit includes blown-up replicas of the manuscript and a self-guided tour with audio guides.
The Long Room in the library also houses 200,000 of the oldest books, including a display for Bram Stoker's Dracula.
The Book of Kells is a national treasure of Ireland and a must-see for visitors to Dublin.
