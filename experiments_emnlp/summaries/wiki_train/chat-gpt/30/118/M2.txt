Brougham Castle near Penrith, Cumbria, England, was built in the early 13th century and is located on the site of an old Roman fort.
The castle, now in the care of English Heritage, features a stone keep with a hall and lord's chamber, as well as a double gatehouse.
Lady Anne Clifford restored the castle in 1643, but it fell into disrepair after her death in 1676.
