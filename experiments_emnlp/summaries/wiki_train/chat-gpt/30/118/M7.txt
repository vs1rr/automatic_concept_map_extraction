Brougham Roman fort (Brocavum) and civil settlement, along with Brougham Castle, are designated as scheduled monuments of national importance.
The fort was constructed during the Roman occupation and was later accompanied by a vicus or civilian settlement.
The castle was built in the medieval period and underwent various modifications over the centuries.
The site has been subject to limited archaeological excavations, revealing well-preserved remains and artifacts.
The history of the area, including the transition from Roman to medieval occupation, is significant for understanding the military and settlement patterns in northern England.
The castle was used as a border defense and later transformed into a country seat by Lady Anne Clifford.
The site has been surveyed and excavated to uncover buried remains and structures, shedding light on the historical significance of Brougham.
