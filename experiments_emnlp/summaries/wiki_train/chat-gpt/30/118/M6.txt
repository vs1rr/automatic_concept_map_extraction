Brougham Castle, located near Penrith, was originally a Roman fort before being taken over by the Norman family of Vieuxpont to build a castle.
The castle later passed to Robert Clifford, who strengthened its defenses during the Scottish wars.
Lady Anne Clifford inherited the estate in 1643 and spent 26 years restoring various castles, including Brougham Castle.
The castle fell into ruin after her death in 1676.
Visitors can explore the impressive ruins, including the oldest part of the castle, the keep.
The castle is situated on the banks of the River Eamont and is cared for by English Heritage.
