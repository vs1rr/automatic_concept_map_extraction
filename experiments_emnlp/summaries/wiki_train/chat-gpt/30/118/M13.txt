Brougham Castle was built in the early 13th century by Robert de Vieuxpont to defend against King John's enemies in the north of England and Scotland.
The castle was strategically located on the rivers Eamont and Lowther, controlling important road crossings.
Over the years, various improvements were made to the castle, including a stone curtain wall and additional buildings by Robert Clifford and his descendants.
Despite these upgrades, the castle was captured and sacked by the Scots in 1388.
It fell into disuse until 1421 when a man was accused of forging coins within its walls.
