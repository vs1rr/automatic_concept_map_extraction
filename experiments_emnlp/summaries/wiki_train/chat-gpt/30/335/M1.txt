The Pendle Witch trials in 1612 were among the most famous in English history, with twelve accused of using witchcraft to murder ten people.
The trials took place at Lancaster and York Assizes, resulting in ten executions and one acquittal.
The trials were documented in Thomas Potts' "The Wonderfull Discoverie of Witches in the Countie of Lancaster."
Six of the accused came from two families, with accusations possibly stemming from competition for livelihood.
