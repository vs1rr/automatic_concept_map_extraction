On August 18, 1612, the trials of the Pendle Witches began, with nine Lancashire women and two men accused of various murders.
Twelve people were charged, with one found not guilty, one dying in prison, and the other ten being executed by hanging.
The trials were well-documented in British history, thanks to a published account by Thomas Potts.
