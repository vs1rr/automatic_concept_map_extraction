The Byzantine Empire, also known as the Eastern Roman Empire, thrived for 1000 years after the collapse of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE.
Under Emperor Constantine I, the capital city of Byzantium was renamed Constantinople and the empire embraced Christianity.
The peak of the empire occurred during the Justinian Dynasty, with Emperor Justinian I expanding territory, establishing reforms, and funding public works projects such as the Hagia Sophia.
The empire split from the Catholic Church in 1054 CE and fought against Muslim forces for control of the eastern Mediterranean.
In 1453, Constantinople fell to the Ottoman Empire, leading to the end of the Byzantine Empire.
Fun facts about the empire include its focus on religious art, the change of the official language from Latin to Greek, and Emperor Justinian granting women the right to buy and own land.
