Caleb Haskell joined Benedict Arnold's expedition to Quebec during the Revolutionary War, believing it was a good idea to prevent the British from launching military operations from there.
The expedition, which began in the fall of 1775, did not go as planned.
Arnold's men traveled twice as far as intended through wilderness in Maine, enduring hardships such as leaking boats and dwindling supplies.
The siege of Quebec ultimately failed.
