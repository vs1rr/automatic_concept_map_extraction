In 1775, William Pierce, a soldier from Hadley, Massachusetts, joined Benedict Arnold's expedition to Quebec.
He kept a journal of his experiences, which was published anonymously.
After 237 years, researchers Stephen Burk and Stephen Darley were able to uncover William Pierce's identity as the author of the journal.
Through genealogical research, they confirmed that William's brother, David, died on the expedition, as described in the journal.
William Pierce's tombstone lies in the Old Hadley Cemetery, but he never married or had children.
His journal sheds light on the hardships faced by the soldiers on the expedition, including harsh weather and lack of food.
The discovery of William Pierce's identity highlights the sacrifices made by American patriots during the Revolutionary War.
