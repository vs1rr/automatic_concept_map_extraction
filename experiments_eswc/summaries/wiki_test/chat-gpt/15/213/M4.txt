President William McKinley was assassinated on September 14, 1901 by anarchist Leon Czolgosz at the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo, New York.
Despite initial hopes for his recovery, McKinley died from complications of the bullet wounds.
Vice President Theodore Roosevelt assumed the presidency following McKinley's death.
Czolgosz was arrested, tried, found guilty, and executed for the assassination.
The funeral procession for McKinley traveled from Buffalo to Washington, DC, and then to Ohio, where he was buried.
The event sparked widespread mourning and public reactions, as documented in historic newspapers available in the Chronicling America digital
