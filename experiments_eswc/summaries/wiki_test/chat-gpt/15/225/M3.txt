The Chalukya Dynasty was a prominent royal dynasty that ruled southern and central India from the 6th to the 12th century.
They ruled as three related but individual dynasties, with the Badami Chalukyas being the earliest.
The dynasty saw a period of political and architectural development, with the rise of efficient administration, overseas trade, and the birth of Chalukyan architecture.
The Chalukyas were known for their organized army, powerful navy, and construction of over 100 buildings, including temples and palaces.
