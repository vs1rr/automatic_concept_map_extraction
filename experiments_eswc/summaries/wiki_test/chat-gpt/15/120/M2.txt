Jesus College, Oxford is one of the colleges of the University of Oxford, founded in 1571 by Queen Elizabeth I. Initially established for the education of clergy, the college now offers a broad range of secular subjects.
The college's founder, Hugh Price, played a significant role in its establishment.
The oldest buildings date back to the 16th and 17th centuries, with further additions made in subsequent centuries.
The English Civil War disrupted college life, but stability was restored by Leoline Jenkins in the 17th century.
The 18th and 19th centuries saw a decline in academic standards, but reforms in the latter half of the 19th century led to improvements.
Women were first admitted in 1974, and the college now has a diverse student population.
Notable alumni include Harold Wilson, T. E. Lawrence, and Norman Washington Manley.
The college has a strong Welsh connection, with a significant number of Welsh students and scholars associated with it.
The college's coat of arms features three stags on a green background, and its traditional graces are said before and after formal dinners.
The college also has a strong rowing tradition, with the Jesus College Boat Club being a prominent part of student life.
Overall, Jesus College, Oxford has a rich history and continues to be a prestigious institution within the University of Oxford.
