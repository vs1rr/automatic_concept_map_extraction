The British government released documents under the 30-year rule regarding the conflict in Ireland, specifically the 1981 hunger strikes.
The papers reveal Margaret Thatcher's involvement and show a policy of inflexibility and intransigence.
The documents also mention a claim that Bobby Sands offered to suspend the hunger strike for five days, which is disputed.
The British government refused to negotiate political status with the hunger strikers.
The papers also detail the "back-channel" communication between a Derry contact and a British intelligence agent, raising questions about their relationship.
The hunger strike had far-reaching repercussions on individuals, families, and the political landscape.
