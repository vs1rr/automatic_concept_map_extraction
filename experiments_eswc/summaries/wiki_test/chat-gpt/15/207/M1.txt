Newly released documents reveal Margaret Thatcher's private regrets over the 1981 Irish hunger strike and her fears following the 1984 Brighton bombing.
The files show that Thatcher expressed deep concern for those affected by the hunger strike but refused a meeting with two mothers, believing it would not help.
After narrowly avoiding assassination in the bombing, Thatcher feared further attacks by the IRA and worried that the incident would derail secret peace negotiations in Northern Ireland.
The documents also show Thatcher's reluctance to continue negotiations with the Republic of Ireland after the bombing, as she was pessimistic about their outcome.
