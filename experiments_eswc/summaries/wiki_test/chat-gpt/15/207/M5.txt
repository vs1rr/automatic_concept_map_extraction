The hunger strikes in Northern Ireland in 1981 had a significant impact on the political landscape.
Republican prisoners, led by Bobby Sands, went on hunger strike to demand political status, resulting in 10 deaths.
The strikes garnered international sympathy and support for the IRA and Sinn Féin.
Sands was elected as an MP, and two other hunger strikers were elected as TDs in the Republic of Ireland.
The strikes led to the British government making concessions to meet some of the prisoners' demands.
