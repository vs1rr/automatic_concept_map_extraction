Belton House in Lincolnshire has a rich history dating back to medieval times when it was owned by St Mary's Abbey at York.
After the Dissolution of the Monasteries, the land reverted back to the Crown.
In the late 17th century, Sir John Brownlow built a new country house on the estate.
The house underwent various renovations and refurbishments over the years, showcasing a mix of architectural styles.
The property features a vast collection of art, furniture, and porcelain, as well as beautifully maintained gardens and parkland.
