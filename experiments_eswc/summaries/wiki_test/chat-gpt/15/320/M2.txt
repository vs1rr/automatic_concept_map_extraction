Edward VIII became King of England in 1936 but abdicated the throne in December of the same year in order to marry Wallis Simpson, a twice-divorced American woman.
The British government, media, and Church of England all played a role in influencing Edward's decision to abdicate.
The government did not approve of Simpson due to suspicions of her being an agent for Nazi Germany and her perceived unsuitability as a consort.
The media also played a crucial role in shaping public opinion against the marriage.
Edward's abdication led to his brother George VI becoming king, while Edward was given the title Duke
