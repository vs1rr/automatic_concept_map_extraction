Spencer Perceval, an unlikely candidate for political honors, was the seventh son of the 2nd Earl of Egmont.
He had to make his own way after his father's death when he was ten.
Perceval studied at Harrow and Cambridge, and then practiced as a barrister.
He had modest success until his connections and hard work improved his prospects.
He held various legal positions, including solicitor-general and attorney-general, before becoming chancellor of the Exchequer and leader of the House.
Perceval was known for his defense of the Anglican Church and his opposition to Catholic emancipation.
He faced challenges in his administration, including the Walcheren expedition and the Regency crisis.
Perceval was assassinated in 1812, leaving behind a legacy of integrity and dedication to his office.
