Ancient Egypt was divided into several time periods, starting with the Early Dynastic Period from 3100-2600 B.C. and ending with the Greco-Roman Period from 332 B.C. to 395 A.D. Each period had its own dynasties and powerful rulers, such as Cheops and Ramesses II.
The Intermediate Periods saw struggles with central government control, while the Greco-Roman Period marked a time of foreign rule in Egypt.
