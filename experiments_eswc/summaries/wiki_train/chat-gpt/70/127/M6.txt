Medieval castles were fortified structures that offered protection to nobility and other classes of people in Europe during the 9th and 10th centuries.
They were built to control surrounding areas, protect families and wealth, and coordinate raids on other castles, towns, and villages.
Castles were divided into those owned by the king and Anglo-Norman lords, with the best protection offered by the king's castles.
Urban castles were enforced by sheriffs to maintain peace.
Castle designs evolved over time, with Normans introducing sophisticated designs.
Strategic locations ensured survival, while concentric castles with multiple outer walls became popular for defense.
Early medieval castles were easier to construct, with Motte and Bailey designs being common.
Famous medieval castles were mainly built during the Norman period.
Castle entertainment was important for keeping staff in good spirits.
Castle life varied for different levels of society, with knights living in luxury while lower-level staff faced challenges.
Castles required a large number of troops for defense, including knights, guards, and archers.
Castle parts included arrow slits, battlements, curtain walls, the keep, gatehouse, and moat.
Castle sieges were common, with siege weapons like battering rams and trebuchets used.
Famous English castles include Warwick and Windsor Castle.
Norman castles were built after the Norman conquest of England in 1066.
Motte and Bailey castles were quick to build.
European castles first appeared in Normandy, France, with Motte and Bailey designs.
Castle parts were similar across Europe, including arrow slits, battlements, curtain walls, the keep, gatehouse, and moat.
Famous medieval castles include Chateau Gaillard, Cite de Carcassonne, Leeds Castle, Windsor Castle, and the Tower of London.
Today, many castles are tourist attractions, offering guided tours and historical information about their medieval past.
Restoration has been necessary for damaged castles, preserving their historical significance.
Castles serve as reminders of the darker ages of war and conflict.
