Brougham Castle, located just outside Penrith in Westmorland, has a rich history dating back to a Roman fort on the site.
The castle was built by the Norman family of Vieuxpont and later passed to the Clifford family, who played a significant role in the Scottish wars.
Lady Anne Clifford inherited the castle in 1643 and spent years restoring it and other castles in the area.
After her death in 1676, the castle fell into ruin.
Visitors can explore the impressive ruins, including the oldest part of the castle, the keep, which dates back to 1175.
The keep features various rooms, including the lord's chamber where Lady Anne slept and died.
The castle offers stunning views of the surrounding area, including the River Eamont and the A66.
The site is managed by English Heritage and includes a small shop and an exhibition of Roman tombstones from a nearby fort.
A guidebook is available for visitors to learn more about the history of Brougham Castle and its restoration by Lady Anne Clifford.
Overall, Brougham Castle is a fascinating historical site with a picturesque location along the River Eamont, offering visitors a glimpse into the castle's past and the efforts to preserve its heritage.
