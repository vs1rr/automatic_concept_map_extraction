This walk takes participants from London to the rural countryside of East Sussex, where they will explore the moated ruins of Bodiam Castle, built in 1385.
The walk begins in Robertsbridge, a village with historic homes and a cricket bat factory.
Participants will follow an old railway line, passing pillboxes from World War II, before stopping for lunch at The Castle Inn in Bodiam.
The highlight of the day is a tour of Bodiam Castle, where visitors can climb the turrets for a view of the Rother River Valley.
The return journey includes a stop at the Salehurst Halt pub before heading back to Robertsbridge and catching a train back to London.
The walk includes a guided tour, admission to the castle, lunch, and roundtrip rail fare from London.
Prices vary based on group size, with discounts available for larger groups.
Reservations can be made by contacting the walk leaders via phone or email, providing information on the desired walk, dates, group size, and contact details.
