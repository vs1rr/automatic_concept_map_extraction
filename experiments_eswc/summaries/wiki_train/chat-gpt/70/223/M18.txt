The Byzantine Empire reached its height under the Macedonian emperors in the late 9th to early 11th centuries, with a revival of intellectual and artistic life.
The empire faced challenges from the Holy Roman Empire, the Seljuk Turks, and the Fourth Crusade, which led to the sack of Constantinople in 1204.
The empire briefly revived under the Palaeologan dynasty, but ultimately fell to the Ottoman Turks in 1453.
Byzantine art, including mosaics, frescoes, illuminated manuscripts, ivories, and enamels, had a lasting impact on European art and culture.
Byzantine architecture evolved from Roman architecture, with a focus on geometric complexity, domes, and mosaics.
The Byzantine Empire's legacy continued in the Russian Empire and the Eastern Orthodox Church.
