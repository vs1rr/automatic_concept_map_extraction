The Byzantine Empire, surrounded by enemies such as the Bulgarian Empire, the Serbian Empire, and the Turks, faced a dire situation.
In order to receive aid from the West against the Turks, the Byzantines were forced to convert to Catholicism, sparking riots among the Orthodox populace who despised the Catholics.
Despite the conversion, the Ottomans defeated most of the Empire, except for Constantinople.
On May 29, 1453, Constantinople fell to the Ottoman Turks, marking the end of the Byzantine Empire.
The city was transformed into the Islamic city of Istanbul.
The rise of the Ottoman Empire, led by Osman, united the Turks and posed a significant threat to the Byzantines.
The Byzantine Empire's acceptance of Catholic Christianity in 1439 at the Council of Florence was met with opposition from the populace.
The fall of Constantinople in 1453 marked the end of the Byzantine Empire and the beginning of the Ottoman Empire's dominance in the region.
