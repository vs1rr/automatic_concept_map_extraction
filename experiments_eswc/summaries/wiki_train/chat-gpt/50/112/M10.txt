The Book of Kells is a famous illuminated manuscript written over 1,200 years ago by monks in medieval Ireland.
It is named after the village of Kells where it was kept for centuries.
The book contains the four gospels in Latin and is known for its intricate insular majuscule script and colorful illuminations.
The original Book of Kells is housed in Trinity College Library in Dublin, with exact facsimile replicas available for purchase.
The Hackley Public Library in Muskegon, Michigan, fundraised to acquire a replica for their collection.
The replica is displayed in a special case with the names of donors listed.
It is believed to be the only facsimile edition of the Book of Kells on permanent display in a public library in North America.
The pages of the replica are turned weekly, and a video and commentary volume are available for those interested in learning more about the book.
Additional information on the Book of Kells can be found on various websites.
