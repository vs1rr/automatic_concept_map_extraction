Trinity College in Dublin, Ireland, is the oldest university in the country and one of the seven ancient universities of Britain and Ireland.
The college, founded in 1592, is synonymous with the University of Dublin and is known for its famous library, which houses the renowned Book of Kells.
The Book of Kells is an illuminated manuscript Gospel book created around 800 AD in a Columbian monastery in either Britain or Ireland.
It contains the four Gospels of the New Testament, prefatory texts, and intricate decorations that combine traditional Christian iconography with Celtic motifs.
The manuscript is considered a masterpiece of Western calligraphy and is widely regarded as Ireland's finest national treasure.
The illustrations and ornamentation in the Book of Kells are known for their extravagance and complexity, featuring figures of humans, animals, mythical beasts, Celtic knots, and vibrant colors.
