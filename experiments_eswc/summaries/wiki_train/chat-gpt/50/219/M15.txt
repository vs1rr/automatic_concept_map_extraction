During World War 2, British scientists joined the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos to help develop the atomic bomb in a race against the Germans.
This book highlights the vital contributions of the British Mission to the project, which have been largely overlooked.
The collaboration between British, American, and Canadian scientists had a profound impact on shaping the nuclear programs of the United States, Great Britain, and the USSR after the war.
