The Byzantine Empire, also known as the Eastern Roman Empire, lasted for 1000 years after the fall of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE.
Its capital city, Constantinople, was the largest and wealthiest city in Europe during this time.
Emperor Constantine
I established Constantinople as the capital and embraced Christianity, which became a significant part of the empire for the next millennium.
The peak of the Byzantine Empire occurred during the Justinian Dynasty, with Emperor Justinian I expanding the empire's territory and implementing reforms, including the creation of the Justinian Code.
The empire also fought against Muslims for control of the eastern Mediterranean and eventually fell to the Ottoman Empire in 1453.
Some fun facts about the Byzantine Empire include its focus on religious art, the change of the official language from Latin to Greek, and Emperor Justinian granting women the right to buy and own land.
The impact of Roman rule, from the early Republic to the Byzantine Empire, lasted for nearly 2000 years in Europe.
Constantinople, now known as Istanbul, is the largest city in Turkey.
