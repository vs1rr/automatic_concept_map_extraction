The restored Byzantine Empire faced numerous enemies, including the Bulgarian Empire, the Serbian Empire, and the Turks who overran Asia Minor.
In order to receive aid from the West against the Turks, the Byzantines converted to Catholicism, sparking riots among the Orthodox populace.
Despite their efforts, Constantinople fell to the Ottoman Turks in 1453, leading to the end of the Byzantine Empire.
The Ottomans established the Ottoman Empire, transforming Constantinople into the Islamic city of Istanbul.
The Byzantines' acceptance of Catholic Christianity at the Council of Florence in 1439 was met with opposition from their congregations, and the fall of Constantinople marked the end of an era.
