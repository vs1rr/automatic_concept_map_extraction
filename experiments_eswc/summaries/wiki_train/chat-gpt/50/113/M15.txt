The reviewer describes their visit to Borobudur, the largest Buddhist monument in the world, located on the island of Java.
They arrived early in the morning to witness the sunrise, which is said to be a stunning sight.
The temple, built in the 9th century, consists of nine stacked platforms decorated with bas-relief panels and Buddha statues.
The structure is massive and impressive, evoking a sense of grandeur.
The temple's architecture shows influences of Indian Gupta art and traditional Indonesian art.
The pilgrimage through the temple represents three levels of Buddhist spirituality.
The reviewer notes the mixed Hindu and Buddhist influences in the temple's design, with depictions of daily life, Buddha's life, and various figures.
The temple was abandoned in the 14th century and rediscovered in the 19th century.
It has suffered damage from natural disasters and terrorist attacks but remains a UNESCO World Heritage site.
The reviewer recommends visiting early in the morning to avoid the heat and crowds.
They describe the experience as special and blessed, similar to visiting other famous sites like Angkor Wat.
Overall, the reviewer had a positive experience at Borobudur and recommends it as a must-visit destination for those interested in history and spirituality.
