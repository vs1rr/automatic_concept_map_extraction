The reviewer describes their visit to Borobudur, the largest Buddhist monument in the world, located on the island of Java.
They arrived at the Manohara resort before sunrise to witness the stunning sunrise over the temple.
The temple, built in the 9th century, consists of nine stacked platforms decorated with bas-relief panels and Buddha statues.
The structure is a marvel of architecture, built without cement or mortar.
The pilgrimage through the temple represents three levels of Buddhist spirituality.
The temple reflects a mix of Hindu and Buddhist influences, with bas-reliefs depicting daily life in Java and the life of Buddha.
The temple was abandoned in the 14th century and rediscovered in the 19th century.
It has suffered damage from earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and bomb attacks.
Despite these challenges, the temple remains a UNESCO World Heritage site and a place of pilgrimage for Buddhists in Indonesia.
The reviewer recommends visiting early in the morning to avoid the heat and crowds, and describes the experience as special and blessed.
They also note the importance of preserving the temple's cultural and historical significance.
