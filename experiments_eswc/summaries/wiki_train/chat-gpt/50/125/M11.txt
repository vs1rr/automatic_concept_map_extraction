Capon Chapel is a historic church in West Virginia that has served as a place of worship since the mid-19th century.
Originally used by Baptists, it later became a Methodist church and is now part of the United Methodist Church.
The church cemetery contains the remains of Civil War veterans and African Americans.
Capon Chapel was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2012 for its rural religious architecture and significance as a rural church in Hampshire County.
