Medieval castles were fortified structures that offered protection to nobility and other classes of people in Europe during the 9th and 10th centuries.
They were built to control surrounding areas, protect families and wealth from rivals, and coordinate raids on other castles, towns, and villages.
Castles were divided into those owned by the king and Anglo-Norman lords, with the former offering the best protection.
Urban castles, also owned by the king, were enforced by sheriffs to maintain peace.
The Normans were master castle builders who introduced many designs, with concentric castles becoming popular in the late medieval period.
Early medieval castles were easier to construct, with the Motte and Bailey design being popular.
Famous medieval castles were mainly built during the Norman period in England.
Castle entertainment was important to keep staff in good spirits.
Castles were strategically located in areas like mountain passes or near rivers for survival.
Castle moats were around 12 feet wide and 30 feet deep.
Castle defenses were crucial, with various weapons used during sieges.
Castle staff lived in the castles permanently and provided labor to keep them running.
Famous English castles include Warwick and Windsor Castle.
Norman castles were built after the Norman conquest of England in 1066.
Motte and Bailey castles were quick and easy to build.
Renowned masons and architects like Gundulf and Robert de Belleme designed many medieval castles.
Castles were built for defense and to maintain power and wealth.
The earliest castles were built in strategic positions for protection.
Castles were used to store wealth and resources, with early forms of fortifications originating in the Fertile Crescent and Egypt.
Castles were built to house military units and nobility.
Siege tactics were used to take over castles.
European medieval castles first appeared in Normandy, France, with the Motte and Bailey design being popular.
Castle parts included arrow slits, battlements, curtain walls, the castle keep, gatehouse, and moat.
Famous medieval castles include Chateau Gaillard, Cite de Carcassonne, Leeds Castle, Windsor Castle, and the Tower of London.
Today, castles are mainly tourist attractions, with many being restored for historical purposes.
They serve as reminders of the darker ages of war and conflict.
