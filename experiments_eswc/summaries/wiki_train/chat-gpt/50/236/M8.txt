For 81 years, Brazil was part of the United Kingdom of Portugal, Brazil, and Algarves, and then became an independent Empire from 1822 to 1889.
The Portuguese court moved to Rio de Janeiro to escape Napoleon's troops, leading to Brazil's elevation from a colony to a kingdom.
When the king tried to return to Europe, Brazilians refused to revert to colony status, prompting his son to declare Brazil's independence.
The Orleans-Braganza family controlled both nations, with the Portuguese branch more royalist than their Brazilian cousins.
