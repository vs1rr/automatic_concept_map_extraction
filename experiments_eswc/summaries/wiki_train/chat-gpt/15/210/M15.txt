The Roman province of Egypt, known as Aegyptus, was established in 30 BC under Augustus after defeating Mark Anthony and Cleopatra.
The capital was Alexandria, and the province was divided into small provinces with town councils for administration.
A complex tax system was developed, with land taxes and poll taxes.
Social hierarchy was introduced, with Romans and Greeks at the top.
Egypt was a vital source of food for Rome, and trading flourished in the 1st and 2nd centuries.
