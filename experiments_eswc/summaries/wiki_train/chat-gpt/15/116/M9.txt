Bramshill House and Gardens, built in the early 17th Century by Edward, 11th Baron Zouche of Harringworth, is a large Jacobean 'Prodigy' house with a mix of historic features.
The house has undergone various renovations and additions over the years, including the creation of walled gardens and a man-made lake.
The estate, now owned by the Home Office, includes historic landscape features dating back to medieval times.
