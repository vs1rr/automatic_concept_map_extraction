The windows of the upper story are six feet in width, and ten feet inheight, and are divided into three lights by mullions, with transoms.

Theentablature is enriched with arabesque ornament, and the piercedparapet is three feet in height, with small piers about two feet distantfrom each other.

The whole effect of these details is grand andimposing, in perfect harmony with the general character of the building.
