Indonesia is a country rich in natural attractions, including the famous Borobudur temple, a UNESCO world heritage site.
The temple, built in the 8th century, is the largest Buddhist temple in the world after Angkor Wat in Cambodia.
It consists of nine stacked platforms symbolizing different stages of human life according to Buddhist teachings.
Visitors can enjoy a stunning sunrise view from the temple's top and witness rituals on Vesak day, including the release of flying lanterns.
Other activities at Borobudur include watching traditional dance performances and riding elephants.
The temple is easily accessible from the international airport in Jogjakarta, and visitors can arrange for airport pickup through their hotel.
Overall, Borobudur offers a unique cultural and historical experience for travelers in Indonesia.
