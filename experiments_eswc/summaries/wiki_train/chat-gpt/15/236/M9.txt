Brazilian independence from Portugal was a gradual process that began with King Dom João VI residing in Brazil in 1808.
In 1820, Portugal experienced a Constitutional Revolution, leading to demands for the king's return.
Dom Pedro was left in charge of Brazil when his father returned to Portugal in 1821.
Tensions rose between Pedro and the Portuguese military, leading to his declaration of independence on 9 January 1822.
With the support of key figures like José Bonifácio, Pedro officially broke ties with Portugal on 22 September 1822.
