Tomm Moore, the director of the independent animated film "The Secret of Kells," discusses the inspiration behind the film, which draws from Irish tradition and Celtic aesthetics.
Moore talks about the eclectic and inventive look of the film, influenced by various artists and styles.
He also touches on the importance of storytelling in the film and the value of hand-drawn animation in a world dominated by computer-generated animation.
Moore shares his favorite animated films from his childhood and adulthood, including "My Neighbor Totoro" by Hayao Miyazaki.
He also discusses the special features included in the DVD release of "The Secret of Kells," as well as a prequel graphic novel.
Moore hints at his next project, "Song of the Sea," which explores Irish folklore and the theme of things being lost in a modern world.
He offers words of advice for aspiring animators, emphasizing patience and persistence.
"The Secret of Kells" is available on Blu-ray, DVD, and Netflix, with the comic available online through Comixology.
Moore will be at NY Comic Con for signings and a panel discussion.
