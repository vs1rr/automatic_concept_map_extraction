The Book of Kells is a highly revered illustrated manuscript containing 678 pages made of calf-skin or vellum.
It was likely written in a Columban Monastery on the island of Iona in Scotland and colored using natural dyes by monks.
The book features four symbols - a lion, man, calf, and eagle - which represent the twelve tribes of Israel, not the Apostles as commonly believed.
The Irish people are said to be mostly descended from the tribe of Dan, who arrived in Ireland under various names such as the Tuatha de Danaan.
The Book of Kells also references the four tribal standards mentioned in Christ's Apocalypse/Revelation.
The emblems are linked to the tribes of Israel to whom the Gospels were to be preached.
