Bodiam Castle, a picturesque medieval ruin in England, was built by Sir Edward Dallingridge in the 14th century.
After falling into disrepair, it was rescued and conserved by Lord Curzon and the National Trust.
Visitors can experience medieval life, participate in events, and enjoy tea and cake at the tearoom.
The castle offers interactive activities like A Knight's Peril, where visitors can uncover a murder plot.
Medieval characters roam the ruins, sharing stories and demonstrations.
Events like the Cadbury Easter Egg Trail and Sir Edwards Fair provide entertainment for families.
The castle is open year-round, offering a glimpse into medieval life and history.
