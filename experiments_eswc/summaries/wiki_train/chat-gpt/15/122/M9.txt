Sir William Compton, a Knight born around 1475 in England, was a close friend of King Henry VIII.
He was involved in a scandal with Anne Stafford, the sister of the Duke of Buckingham, which caused tension between Buckingham, Compton, and the King.
Compton was accused of adultery with Anne, but he denied the allegations.
He also arranged a marriage for his daughter, Catherine, which did not take place due to her death.
Compton held important positions in the royal court, including Groom of the Stole.
He died in 1528 from the sweating sickness.
