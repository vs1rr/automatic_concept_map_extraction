The British Empire originated in the late 16th century with chartered companies establishing trading posts in India, the East Indies, Newfoundland, and Hudson Bay.
The empire grew with the development of plantations in the Caribbean and North America, attracting European settlers.
The need for cheap labor led to the African slave trade.
The Navigation Acts restricted colonial trade to British ships.
