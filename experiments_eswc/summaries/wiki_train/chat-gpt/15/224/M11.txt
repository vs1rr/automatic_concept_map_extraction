Stephen Hill, an African American slave, escaped from his owner in the Gold Rush era, prompting a legal battle over his freedom.
Despite California being a free state, federal laws allowed for the capture and return of fugitive slaves.
The state's admission to the Union as a free state in 1850 was a result of a compromise in Congress, but did not extend civil rights to African Americans or Native Americans in California.
The Gold Rush era saw the presence of black slaves in the mines, brought in by white southerners.
The story of Stephen Hill highlights the complexities of slavery and freedom
