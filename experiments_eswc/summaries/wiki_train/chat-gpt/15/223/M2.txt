The Byzantine Empire, also known as the Eastern Roman Empire, thrived for 1000 years after the collapse of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE.
Under Emperor Constantine I, the capital city of Byzantium was renamed Constantinople, which became the largest and wealthiest city in Europe.
The peak of the empire occurred during the Justinian Dynasty, with Emperor Justinian I expanding territory and establishing reforms, including the Justinian Code.
