The CSI Effect theory suggests that jurors who watch crime dramas like CSI have higher expectations of scientific evidence in criminal trials, but a study by Donald E. Shelton found no link between watching such shows and being less likely to convict without scientific evidence.
The high expectations of scientific evidence in trials can be attributed to broader cultural changes and misconceptions about the accuracy and availability of forensic science technology.
While DNA analysis is highly accurate, other forensic science disciplines like fingerprint analysis and hair analysis are unreliable and lack scientific validity.
Despite the inaccuracies portrayed in crime dramas, they have inspired interest in forensic science and even led to the creation of educational exhibits like CSI:
The Experience.
