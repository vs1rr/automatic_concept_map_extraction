The CSI effect refers to the unrealistic public perception of forensic science's effectiveness and availability, influenced by crime scene TV dramas like CSI.
Jurors now demand scientifically unattainable forensic proof due to these shows, altering prosecutorial behavior and increasing expectations for forensic evidence.
However, viewers can distinguish between fact and fiction, understanding that TV shows prioritize entertainment over education.
