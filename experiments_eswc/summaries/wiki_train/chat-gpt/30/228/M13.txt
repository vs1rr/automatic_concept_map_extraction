The Long Expedition in June 1819 attempted to conquer Texas, occupying Nacadoches and declaring it an independent Republic.
Spanish troops drove them back to Louisiana in Fall 1821.
James Long was captured and killed in Mexico City.
Mexican Constitution of 1824 established Coahuila y
Tejas with three branches of government and encouraged immigration.
The Law of April 6, 1830 restricted immigration from the US.
The Battle of Velasco in June 1832 led to Mexican surrender.
The Convention of 1832 and 1833 aimed for Texas statehood, but failed
