The Byzantines, who bridged the Classical and Post-Classical era, were a powerful economic force in the Mediterranean for nearly a thousand years.
Their capital, Constantinople, was a center of religious building and art, with the Hagia Sophia being a notable masterpiece.
The Byzantine Empire reached its greatest extent in the 6th century under Justinian, but eventually fell to the Turks in 1453.
The Turks, who had a history spanning over 4,000 years, settled in Anatolia and established the Anatolian Seljuk State after defeating the Byzantines at the Battle of Malazgirt in 1071.
The collapse of the Seljuk Empire led to the emergence of various Turkish beyliks, one of which was the Ottoman Beylik.
