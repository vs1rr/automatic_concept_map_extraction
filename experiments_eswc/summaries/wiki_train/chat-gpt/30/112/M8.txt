The Old Library in Dublin, Ireland houses the famous Book of Kells, along with other ancient manuscripts such as the Book of Armagh and the Book of Mulling.
The Book of Kells, believed to have originated from the monastery on Iona, is known for its intricate decoration and historical significance.
Despite surviving Viking raids and being moved to Dublin for safekeeping during the Cromwellian Wars, the Book of Kells remains a remarkable example of illuminated manuscripts.
The library also features the Long Room, a stunning space filled with oak bookcases holding a vast collection of British and Irish publications.
Additionally, visitors can see a fifteenth-century harp and an original printing of the 1916 Proclamation of the Irish Republic.
