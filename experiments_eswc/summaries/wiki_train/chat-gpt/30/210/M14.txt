Ptolemy I Soter, also known as Ptolemy Lagides, was a Macedonian general under Alexander the Great who became ruler of Egypt and founded a dynasty that ruled for three centuries.
He assimilated to Egyptian culture, assuming the title of pharaoh.
Ptolemy was one of Alexander's most trusted generals and played a key role in many campaigns.
After Alexander's death, Ptolemy became satrap of Egypt and engaged in various wars and rivalries with other Diadochi.
He successfully defended Egypt against invaders and expanded his territory to include Cyprus and parts of Syria.
Ptolemy was known for his shrewdness, patronage of letters, and founding of the Great Library of Alexandria.
He died in 283 BC at the age of 84.
Ptolemy also sponsored the mathematician Euclid and appears in various fictional portrayals in literature and film.
