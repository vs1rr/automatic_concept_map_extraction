The reviewer describes their early morning visit to Borobudur, the largest Buddhist monument in the world, located in Java.
They arrived before sunrise to witness the stunning views and architectural marvel of the temple.
The temple, built in the 9th century, consists of stacked platforms decorated with bas-relief panels and Buddha statues.
The structure reflects a mix of Hindu and Buddhist influences, with depictions of daily life, Buddha's life, and Hindu figures.
The temple was abandoned in the 14th century and rediscovered in the 19th century.
It has suffered damage from natural disasters and terrorist attacks.
Despite these challenges, the temple remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a place of pilgrimage for Buddhists.
The reviewer recommends visiting early in the morning to avoid the heat and crowds, and describes the experience as special and blessed.
They also mention the importance of preserving the temple's cultural and historical significance.
Overall, the reviewer had a positive experience at Borobudur and recommends it as a must-visit destination for those interested in history and spirituality.
