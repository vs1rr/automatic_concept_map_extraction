Rumors of gold in California had circulated for years before the Gold Rush, but it wasn't until gold was discovered at Sutter's Mill that the rush began.
John Sutter, a Swiss emigrant, received a land grant in Sacramento Valley and built Sutter's Fort, which became a rest station for travelers.
In 1848, James Marshall found gold at the mill, sparking a frenzy as word spread across the country.
