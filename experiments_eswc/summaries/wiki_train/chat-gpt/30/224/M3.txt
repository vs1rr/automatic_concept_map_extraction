The California Gold Rush was a period of mass hysteria and migration in American history sparked by the discovery of gold in Northern California in 1848.
Despite the initial excitement, most people only found enough gold to cover daily expenses.
The rush began at Sutter's Mill when James W. Marshall found a gold nugget, leading to a wave of immigration by "49ers" seeking fortune.
The influx strained infrastructure, leading to crime and vigilante justice.
The Gold Rush prompted development in California and the building of the Panama Railway.
San Francisco initially became a ghost town before booming with returning miners.
The rush ended in 1858 with the start of the New Mexican Gold Rush.
