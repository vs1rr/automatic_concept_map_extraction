A forty-niner is a person, typically a prospector, who went to California in 1849 during the gold rush.
They were known for seeking their fortune in the gold fields.
Examples from historical texts show the experiences and adventures of forty-niners, who often made significant fortunes in California and then returned East to enjoy their wealth.
The term "forty-niner" is associated with the California Gold Rush and the individuals who participated in it, often facing challenges and hardships in their pursuit of gold.
