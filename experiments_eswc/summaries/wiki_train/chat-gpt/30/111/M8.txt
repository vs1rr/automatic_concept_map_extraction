In 1386, the French prepared to invade England by imposing high taxes on nobles, outfitting their fleet extravagantly, and provisioning copious amounts of food.
The fleet was assembled in Sluys, Flanders, with Dutch mercenaries hired for fighting.
The French were confident in their preparations, believing the English were already defeated.
In response, King Richard II raised an army, appointed commissioners to raise militias, manned coastal garrisons, and established lookout posts and barricades.
The English plan was to let the French march inland before destroying their supply ships and confronting them in battle.
