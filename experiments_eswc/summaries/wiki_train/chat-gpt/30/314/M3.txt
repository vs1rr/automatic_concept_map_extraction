The CSI effect is the exaggerated portrayal of forensic science on TV shows, influencing public perception in four ways: understanding of crime scene investigation, depiction of non-existent technologies, unrealistic expectations for evidence, and faster scientific outcomes.
This effect is important as it may impact jury trials by raising expectations for evidence.
It has led to jurors expecting more forensic evidence, increasing confidence in DNA evidence, and more frequent wrongful acquittals.
