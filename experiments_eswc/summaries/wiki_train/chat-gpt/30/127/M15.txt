Harewood Castle, the oldest building at Harewood, underwent a major repair and consolidation project in 2004-2005 with support from English Heritage and the Harewood Estate.
The castle, granted a 'license to crenellate' in 1366, was designed by Sir William de Aldeburgh to balance security and comfort.
The castle featured defensive elements like a portcullis and arrow slit windows, as well as luxurious features such as mullioned windows and internal walkways.
After de Aldeburgh's son's death in 1391, the castle was shared by his daughters and their husbands for over 200 years.
The last resident, Robert Ryther, left in 1630, and by 1657 the castle was uninhabitable.
