Stone castles in Co.
Tipperary date back to the 12th century, initially built from earth and timber before being replaced with stone for defense.
These castles often utilized natural defenses and lacked features like towers and arrow-slits.
Construction required a large workforce, including stone masons, quarrymen, and carpenters.
Despite the use of conquered labor, workers were often unpaid, leading to poverty.
Castles served as offensive and defensive structures, centers of administration, and symbols of power.
They were strategically located near vital resources and travel routes.
Norman invaders established many castles in Tipperary, holding court to maintain control over the local population.
