Castles were built in Europe during Medieval times as fortified structures to defend against foreign invaders and symbolize power.
They were residences for lords and their families, serving as symbols of power and preserving the king's authority over the land.
The emergence of a social and military elite in the Carolingian Empire led to the construction of castles in the ninth and tenth centuries.
When the empire fell, local lords took over administration and built castles to protect their people.
Castles were initially made of wood but later evolved to be made of stone for better fortification.
The keep, a strong tower within the castle, served as a last refuge if outer defenses fell.
Castles were ruled by lords or nobles and were heavily guarded fortresses.
Moats were used as defensive ditches, and castle warfare had advantages due to the difficult access for enemies.
