Stephen Hill and African Americans in the Gold Rush In September 1854 Owen R. Rozier complained to the San Joaquin Republican that his slave, Stephen Spencer Hill, had escaped from the steamer Urilda the night before Rozier and Hill were to set sail for Arkansas.
Court Ruled against Hill California was a free state, but under state and federal laws a fugitive slave could be captured and returned to his owner.
Slavery wasn't popular in the mines, but there were no laws barring it in the early days of the gold rush.
Many of California's delegates were from slave states, but they were also miners.
The slave trade was banned from the District of Columbia and the Fugitive Slave Law was tightened.
