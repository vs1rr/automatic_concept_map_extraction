The text discusses the "CSI Effect" and its impact on juror expectations in criminal trials. It explores whether popular crime television shows like CSI influence jurors to demand scientific evidence for convictions. A study conducted by criminology professors surveyed jurors' expectations for forensic evidence and found that while CSI viewers had higher expectations, it did not significantly affect their verdicts. The text concludes by emphasizing the need for the criminal justice system to adapt to jurors' evolving expectations regarding scientific evidence.
