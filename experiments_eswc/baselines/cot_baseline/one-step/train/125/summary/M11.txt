The text discusses Capon Chapel, a historic church in West Virginia, which has served as a place of worship since the mid-19th century. Originally used by Baptists, it later became a Methodist church and is now used by the United Methodist Church. The church cemetery contains the graves of Civil War veterans and African Americans. Capon Chapel was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2012 for its rural religious architecture and service as a rural church in Hampshire County.
