Ancient Egypt was a civilization centered around the Nile River, with natural boundaries that protected it from invasion. Egyptians worshipped many gods, with special importance given to sun and land deities. The mummification process was used to preserve the dead for their next life. Pyramids were built during the Old Kingdom as part of large complexes for the deceased. The timeline of Ancient Egypt includes periods like the Old Kingdom, Middle Kingdom, and New Kingdom. Egyptian society had a simple structure, with the Pharaoh at the top and slaves at the bottom. Marriage was arranged for young Egyptians, and women had certain rights. Hieroglyphics and hieratic script were used for writing, and Egyptians made advances in various fields like mathematics and technology.
