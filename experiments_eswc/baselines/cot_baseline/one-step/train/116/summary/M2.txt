Bramshill House, a Jacobean mansion in Hampshire, England, has a rich history dating back to Saxon times. It was rebuilt in the 14th century by Thomas Foxley and later completed by Edward Zouche in 1612. The property changed hands over the years, with notable residents including the Cope family and Lord Brocket. After World War II, it housed exiled Romanian royalty. The estate also has a cricket ground that hosted first-class matches in the 19th century.
