Emperor Justinian I, born in 482 CE, is known for his significant military campaigns to reclaim Africa and Italy, as well as his construction projects including the Hagia Sophia. His completion of the Codex Iustinianus is a major achievement. Justinian's early life involved close ties to Emperor Justin I and his military career began in the east. He successfully reconquered Africa from the Vandals and Italy from the Goths. Despite facing challenges such as the Gothic War and the Justinianic Plague, Justinian's reign lasted almost 40 years. Procopius documented his life and achievements, highlighting his legal reforms, conquests, and architectural projects.
