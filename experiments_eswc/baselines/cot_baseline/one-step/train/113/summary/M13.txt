A review of the Borobudur Temple, the largest Buddhist monument in the world, describes the experience of visiting at sunrise. The temple, located in Java, Indonesia, is a massive structure with a rich history dating back to the 9th century. Built without cement, mortar, or glue, it consists of stacked platforms adorned with bas-relief panels and Buddha statues. The temple reflects a blend of Buddhist and Hindu influences, with intricate carvings depicting various aspects of life and spirituality. Despite facing challenges such as abandonment, looting, and natural disasters, Borobudur remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors are advised to explore the temple early in the morning to avoid the heat and witness the stunning sunrise. The site is a place of pilgrimage and celebration for Buddhists in Indonesia, offering a sense of spirituality and awe to all who visit.
