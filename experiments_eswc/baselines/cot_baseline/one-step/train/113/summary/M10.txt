The text discusses the complexities of tourism and its impact on local cultures, focusing on the case of Borobudur Temple in Indonesia. It explores how tourism can both benefit and harm local communities, shaping identities and influencing economic development. The study highlights the challenges faced by the local population, international tourists, and the government in managing the site sustainably. It emphasizes the importance of involving local communities in decision-making processes to ensure the preservation of cultural heritage and equitable distribution of benefits.
