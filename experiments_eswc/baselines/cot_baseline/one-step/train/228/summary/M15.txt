The presentation covers the period of tension between Mexico and Texas from 1823 to 1833. It discusses how the Federalists in Mexico City allowed Texas colonists to live undisturbed, but tensions arose when the Centralists came to power. Events such as the Fredonian Rebellion, the Mier y Teran investigation, the Law of April 6, 1830, the conflict at Anahuac, the Turtle Bayou Resolutions, the Battle of Velasco, and the Conventions of 1832 and 1833 are highlighted. The text also mentions Stephen F. Austin's efforts to seek changes for Texas in Mexico City, his imprisonment, and eventual release, as well as the beginning of reforms in the region.
