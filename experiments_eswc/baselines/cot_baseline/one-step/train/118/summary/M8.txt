Brougham Castle, located near Penrith, is a captivating historical site with roots dating back to a Roman fort. Originally built in the 13th century by the Norman family of Robert de Vieuxpont, the castle saw various enhancements over the years, including stone walls and a gatehouse. The Clifford family later took ownership, with Lady Anne Clifford playing a significant role in its restoration. Despite falling into disrepair in the 17th century, the castle remains a picturesque ruin, inspiring art and literature. Today, Brougham Castle is maintained by English Heritage and offers visitors an informative exhibition and a glimpse into its rich history.
