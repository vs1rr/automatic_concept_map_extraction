Brougham Castle, located beside the River Eamont near Penrith, England, offers a picturesque setting and rich history. Originally built in the early 13th century, the castle incorporates Roman stones from a nearby fort. Over the centuries, it was expanded and fortified by various owners, including the Clifford family. The castle was later restored by Lady Anne Clifford. Visitors can explore the castle towers for impressive views and discover a Roman tombstone fragment within. Nearby attractions include the Countess Pillar and Brougham Hall.
