The text discusses the evolving trends in art conservation education, highlighting a shift towards a more scientific approach over traditional historical and technical knowledge. It examines various undergraduate programs in the United States, critiquing their emphasis on chemistry and materials science at the expense of art history and studio art. The importance of graduate training and practical experience in becoming a qualified art conservator is emphasized, with a focus on the need for a strong foundation in art history. The text also raises concerns about the lack of advanced studio training in graduate programs and the necessity for a more interdisciplinary approach in art conservation education.
