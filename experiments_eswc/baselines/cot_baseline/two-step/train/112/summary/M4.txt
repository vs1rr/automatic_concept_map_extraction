The Book of Kells is a renowned masterpiece of early Christian art, created by Irish monks around 800 CE. It contains the four Gospels of the New Testament in Latin, adorned with intricate illuminations and colorful illustrations. The manuscript, housed at Trinity College Dublin Library, showcases the Hiberno-Saxon style and is a prime example of medieval Christian art. The origins and history of the Book of Kells are linked to monasteries in Iona and Kells, with debates on its creation and authors. The manuscript's elaborate design, influenced by Eastern art, features symbols of the Evangelists, intricate illuminations, and unique narrative illustrations. The legacy of the Book of Kells, along with other Irish illuminated Gospel books, highlights the rich tradition of early Christian art in Ireland, Scotland, and England.
