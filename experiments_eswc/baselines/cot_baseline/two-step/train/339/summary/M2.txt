James Madison, born in 1751, was the eldest of twelve children and received his education in Virginia before attending Princeton University. He became involved in politics during the American Revolution and played key roles in the formation of the new Constitution. Madison served in Congress and as Secretary of State before becoming the fourth President of the United States. His presidency was marked by the War of 1812. After retiring, Madison continued to be involved in politics and passed away in 1836, leaving a legacy as the "Father of the Constitution."
