Brazil's history includes periods as an empire and a federal republic, with significant political and economic developments. The country's colonization by the Portuguese, independence in the early 19th century, and involvement in wars are highlighted. The economy has diversified over time, with Brazil being part of the BRIC countries. Demographics show a mix of white and mulatto populations. In postal history, Brazil issued its first stamps in 1843 and played a role in the development of aviation-related postal services. Airlines like ETA, Condor, and VARIG issued stamps for air services. Zeppelin mail, a unique aspect of postal history, involved special flights and stamps issued by the Luftschiffbau Zeppelin company.
