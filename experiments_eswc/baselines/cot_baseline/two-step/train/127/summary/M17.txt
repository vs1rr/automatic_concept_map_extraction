Medieval warfare during the Middle Ages was not just about battles in open fields, but also involved complex siegecraft. Castles were crucial in asserting power and status, and sieges were a common way to conquer them. Siegecraft evolved over time, involving tactics like blockade, mining, and the use of siege engines like trebuchets and battering rams. Negotiation, bribery, and starvation were also common tactics to force surrender. The article details various siege techniques and the importance of siege warfare in medieval times, highlighting the intricate strategies and technologies used to capture castles.
