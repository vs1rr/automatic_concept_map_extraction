The presentation discusses the tensions between Texas colonists and Mexican authorities from 1823 to 1833. It highlights the conflicts arising from changes in power in Mexico City, including the Fredonian Rebellion, the Mier y Teran investigation, the Law of April 6, 1830, the Anahuac conflict, the Turtle Bayou Resolutions, the Battle of Velasco, and the Conventions of 1832 and 1833. The text also covers Stephen F. Austin's efforts to secure Texas statehood, his imprisonment, and the beginning of reforms in the region.
