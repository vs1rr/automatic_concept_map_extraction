Ptolemy I Soter, a Macedonian general under Alexander the Great, became ruler of Egypt and founded a dynasty that lasted for three centuries. He assimilated to Egyptian culture, claimed descent from Heracles, and was a trusted general of Alexander. Ptolemy strategically secured power in Egypt and neighboring regions, engaging in wars and alliances with other Diadochi. He was known for his shrewdness, patronage of letters, and founding the Great Library of Alexandria. Ptolemy's reign was marked by military successes and political maneuvering, leaving a well-ordered realm at his death.
